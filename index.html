Philosophy

Publications

"Origins of Life Research Does Not Rest on a Mistake." Ergo. Forthcoming.

I defend origins of life research against an argument, given by Roger White in 2007, that it rests on a mistake. More generally, I show how the Bayesian machinery can illuminate the rational search for alternative explanations of improbable data.


Work In Progress

"Of Tornadoes and 747s: In Defense of Defeatism." (Draft available upon request.)

I defend statistical defeatism, as understood and argued against by Matt Kotzen in his "Multiple Studies and Evidential Defeat." I argue that multiple studies can supply evidential defeat, and that to claim otherwise is to endorse paradox.


"How to be a Bayesian Curve Fitter."

This paper has both a negative and a positive aim. The negative aim is to show that we cannot endorse Gordon Belot's argument, given in his "Curve Fitting for Bayesians?" without also abandoning the bulk of modern statistics. The positive aim is to show that the considerations advanced by Belot should nevertheless lead us to abandon countable additivity as a constraint on probabilities.

Projects

Spatial Modelling of Infectious Disease in Texas

My main aim in this project was to determine the effects of poverty and lack of infrastructure, in the South Texas colonias, on rates of 26 infectious diseases. I used Bayesian hierarchical Poisson regression, with conditional autoregressive priors to account for spatial autocorrelation. The below plots show the results for one disease -- amebiasis. Both plots describe the risk ratio (that is, how many times higher the risk) of amebiasis in each Texas county. The left plot includes 95% credible intervals.
